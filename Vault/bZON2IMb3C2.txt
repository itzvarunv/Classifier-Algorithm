The Nile: Lifeblood of the Sands
The fertile banks of the Nile provided the foundation for the entire Egyptian economy and social hierarchy. Without the predictable cycles of the Nile, the ancient Pharaoh would never have had the surplus grain necessary to feed the armies of builders working on the pyramids. Every aspect of ancient life, from religion to agriculture, was a tribute to the river that turned the desert into a lush, thriving civilization.
